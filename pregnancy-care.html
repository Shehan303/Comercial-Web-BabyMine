Pregnancy Care

Supporting you through every stage of your pregnancy journey

First Trimester Weeks 1-12

Your baby's major organs and body systems begin to form during this crucial period.

Baby Development

  • Heart begins to beat (around week 6)
  • Brain and spinal cord form
  • Tiny arms and legs start developing
  • Facial features begin to take shape

Body Changes

  • Breast tenderness
  • Fatigue and tiredness
  • Morning sickness
  • Frequent urination

Nutrition Tips

  • Take prenatal vitamins
  • Eat small, frequent meals
  • Stay hydrated
  • Focus on folate-rich foods

Health Care

  • Schedule first prenatal visit
  • Avoid alcohol and smoking
  • Limit caffeine intake
  • Discuss medications with doctor

Second Trimester Weeks 13-26

Often called the "honeymoon period" of pregnancy with reduced nausea and more energy.

Baby Development

  • Baby's sex becomes apparent
  • Hair, nails and eyelashes form
  • Begins to hear and swallow
  • Develops sleep/wake cycles

Body Changes

  • Visible baby bump appears
  • Increased energy levels
  • Possible backaches
  • Skin changes and stretch marks

Nutrition Tips

  • Increase protein intake
  • Focus on iron-rich foods
  • Include healthy fats
  • Stay hydrated

Health Care

  • Anatomy ultrasound (week 20)
  • Begin prenatal classes
  • Practice good posture
  • Start Kegel exercises

Third Trimester Weeks 27-40

Final preparations as your baby grows and develops for life outside the womb.

Baby Development

  • Rapid weight gain
  • Lungs mature
  • Opens and closes eyes
  • Moves into head-down position

Body Changes

  • Shortness of breath
  • Frequent urination
  • Swollen feet/ankles
  • Braxton Hicks contractions

Nutrition Tips

  • Small, frequent meals
  • Focus on nutrient density
  • Increase fiber intake
  • Continue prenatal vitamins

Health Care

  • Weekly checkups (after 36 weeks)
  • Group B strep test
  • Finalize birth plan
  • Watch for labor signs
